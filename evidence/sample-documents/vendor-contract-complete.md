Confidential Vendor Compliance Agreement
Document ID: TEST-COMPLETE-VENDOR-2026
Classification: Strictly Confidential
Effective Date: August 1, 2026
Expiration Date: August 1, 2027

1. Parties

Party A: Apex Clinical Data Systems Ltd.
Party B: SecureOps Data Center Services Ltd.

Party B will provide on-premises system operations, maintenance, incident support, and security control assistance for Party A. Party B may access customer PII, internal financial records, security configurations, credentials, support scripts, and operational documentation.

2. Confidential Information

Confidential Information includes customer PII, employee data, payment records, internal financial reports, access credentials, architecture diagrams, source snippets, service scripts, security settings, and any information marked confidential by Party A.

3. Service Response Time Requirements

P1 (Critical) Data center outage, data breach risk <=15 minutes <=4 hours
P2 (High) System performance degradation affecting >30% users <=30 minutes <=8 hours
P3 (Medium) Minor system issues, no user impact <=2 hours <=24 hours
P4 (Low) Documentation updates, routine maintenance <=24 hours <=7 business days

4. Data Processing Requirements

Data Localization: All Party A's data must be processed and stored exclusively on Party A's on-premises servers. No data may be transferred to any cloud service or off-site location.

Encryption: All data in transit must use TLS 1.3 encryption; all data at rest must use AES-256 encryption.

Access Control: Party B must apply least privilege access and may only access systems necessary for assigned services.

Data Retention: Party B may not retain any copies of Party A's data after service completion.

Audit Logs: Party B must maintain complete access logs for all data operations for a minimum of 180 days.

5. Data Breach and Security Incident Notification

Party B shall notify Party A in writing within 24 hours after becoming aware of any actual or suspected data breach, security incident, unauthorized access, loss of Confidential Information, or material control failure. The notice must describe affected systems, data categories, mitigation steps, responsible owner, and follow-up remediation plan.

6. Audit Rights

Party A has the right to audit Party B's data processing activities, security controls, access logs, subcontractor activity, and personnel access records upon reasonable notice. Party B shall provide relevant evidence and remediate confirmed findings within the agreed remediation period.

7. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be liable for delay caused by events beyond reasonable control, provided that the affected party promptly notifies the other party and mitigates the impact. Force majeure shall not excuse Party B from confidentiality, data protection, breach notification, access control, return or destruction of data, or incident mitigation obligations.

8. Intellectual Property and Deliverables

Party A shall own all reports, configurations, scripts, documentation, remediation records, and deliverables specifically created for Party A under this agreement. Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property. Party B grants Party A a perpetual, royalty-free license to use any Party B background materials embedded in deliverables solely as necessary to operate and maintain Party A systems.

9. Security Reporting

Party B shall provide Party A with a monthly security report covering access review results, privileged account changes, incident statistics, subcontractor activity, unresolved remediation items, and confirmation that data localization, encryption, access control, and retention obligations remain satisfied.

10. Confidentiality

Party B shall not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without Party A's written consent. Party B shall return or securely destroy Confidential Information upon termination. Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of this agreement for a period of 3 years.

11. Liability

In the event of a breach of confidentiality by Party B, Party B shall pay liquidated damages equal to 20% of the total contract value. Party B shall also compensate Party A for direct and indirect losses caused by the breach.

12. Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising from this agreement shall first be resolved through negotiation. If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be submitted to the Shanghai Arbitration Commission.

13. Miscellaneous

This agreement is governed by the laws of the People's Republic of China. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
