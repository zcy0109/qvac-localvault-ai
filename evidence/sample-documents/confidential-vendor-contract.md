Background
Party A engages Party B to provide on-premises data center operation and maintenance services. During the service period, Party B will have access to Party A's customer personal information, financial records, and internal business data. This agreement governs the confidentiality and security of all such sensitive information.
2. Definition of Confidential Information
Confidential Information includes but is not limited to:
Customer PII (names, phone numbers, email addresses, payment information)
Party A's internal financial statements and budget plans
Data center architecture diagrams and security configurations
All technical documents, code snippets, and system credentials provided by Party A
Any oral or written information marked as "confidential" during the cooperation
3. Vendor Core Obligations
3.1 Service Response Time Requirements

3.2 Personnel Management
All Party B staff accessing Party A's premises must pass background checks
Staff must sign individual confidentiality agreements before onboarding
Party B must provide a complete list of authorized personnel to Party A monthly
4. Data Processing Requirements
Data Localization: All Party A's data must be processed and stored exclusively on Party A's on-premises servers. No data may be transferred to any cloud service or off-site location.
Encryption: All data in transit must use TLS 1.3 encryption; all data at rest must use AES-256 encryption.
Access Control: Implement least privilege access principle. Staff may only access data necessary for their specific job functions.
Data Retention: Party B may not retain any copies of Party A's data after service completion.
Audit Logs: Party B must maintain complete access logs for all data operations for a minimum of 180 days.
5. Subcontractor Management
Party B may not subcontract any part of the services without prior written approval from Party A
All approved subcontractors must sign confidentiality agreements identical to this agreement
Party B remains fully liable for any breaches committed by its subcontractors
6. Confidentiality Obligations
Party B shall not disclose any confidential information to any third party without Party A's written consent
Confidential information may only be accessed by authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis
Party B shall not reproduce, copy, or distribute confidential information except as necessary for service provision
All confidential information must be returned or securely destroyed upon termination of this agreement
Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of this agreement for a period of 3 years
7. Liability for Breach
In the event of a breach of confidentiality by Party B, Party B shall pay liquidated damages equal to 20% of the total contract value
Party B shall also compensate Party A for all direct and indirect losses resulting from the breach
Party A reserves the right to terminate this agreement immediately upon discovery of any breach

Any disputes arising from this agreement shall be resolved through friendly negotiation. If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be submitted to the Shanghai Arbitration Commission for arbitration in accordance with its rules.
